**Last updated: June 20, 2026**

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between Notra, Inc. ("Notra", "we", "us", or "our") and the customer using Notra ("Customer", "you", or "your") for the use of Notra's website, dashboard application, APIs, integrations, automations, AI content-generation features, and related services (the "Service").

This DPA is intended to satisfy Article 28 of the GDPR and applies when Notra processes Customer Personal Data on behalf of Customer as a processor, service provider, or equivalent role under Data Protection Laws.

## 1. Definitions

**Agreement** means the Terms of Service, order form, subscription agreement, or other written agreement governing Customer's use of the Service.

**Customer Personal Data** means personal data, personal information, or equivalent information that Customer submits to the Service or makes available to Notra through the Service, including through connected integrations, and that Notra processes on behalf of Customer.

**Data Protection Laws** means all privacy and data protection laws applicable to the processing of Customer Personal Data under the Agreement, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 ("GDPR"), the UK GDPR, the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, and applicable United States privacy laws.

**Security Incident** means a breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Customer Personal Data processed by Notra.

**Standard Contractual Clauses** or **SCCs** means the standard contractual clauses adopted by the European Commission in Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, as updated or replaced from time to time.

Terms such as "controller", "processor", "data subject", "personal data", "processing", and "supervisory authority" have the meanings given to them under applicable Data Protection Laws.

## 2. Roles of the Parties

For Customer Personal Data, Customer is the controller or processor, and Notra is the processor or subprocessor, as applicable.

Customer determines the purposes and means of processing Customer Personal Data, including which data is submitted to the Service, which integrations are connected, which users are invited, and which content-generation workflows are run.

Notra processes Customer Personal Data only on Customer's behalf and in accordance with this DPA, the Agreement, Customer's documented instructions, and applicable law.

## 3. Details of Processing

The subject matter, nature, purpose, duration, data categories, and data subject categories are described in **Annex 1: Details of Processing**.

Customer is responsible for ensuring that Customer has a valid legal basis and all required rights, notices, permissions, and consents to submit Customer Personal Data to the Service and to instruct Notra to process it.

## 4. Customer Instructions

Customer instructs Notra to process Customer Personal Data as necessary to:

- provide, secure, maintain, and improve the Service;
- authenticate users and manage accounts, workspaces, permissions, API keys, and sessions;
- process Customer content, prompts, connected integration data, and generated outputs;
- operate automations, schedules, notifications, and workflows configured by Customer;
- provide support, troubleshooting, billing, usage tracking, analytics, and abuse prevention;
- comply with applicable law and enforce the Agreement.

Customer's instructions include this DPA, the Agreement, Customer's use and configuration of the Service, and any written instructions mutually agreed by the parties.

Notra will inform Customer if, in Notra's reasonable opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws, unless applicable law prohibits Notra from doing so.

## 5. Confidentiality

Notra will ensure that personnel authorized to process Customer Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

## 6. Security Measures

Notra will implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Customer Personal Data against Security Incidents and to preserve the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of the Service.

The current measures are described in **Annex 2: Technical and Organizational Measures**. Customer acknowledges that security measures may be updated over time, provided that Notra does not materially reduce the overall security of the Service.

## 7. Subprocessors

Customer grants Notra general authorization to engage subprocessors to process Customer Personal Data for the purposes described in this DPA.

Notra's current subprocessors are listed at [usenotra.com/subprocessors](/subprocessors). Notra will update that page when it adds or replaces subprocessors that may process Customer Personal Data.

Notra will impose data protection obligations on subprocessors that are no less protective in substance than those in this DPA, to the extent applicable to the subprocessors' services. Notra remains responsible for its subprocessors' performance of their data protection obligations.

Customer may object to a new subprocessor on reasonable data protection grounds by contacting Notra at dominik@usenotra.com within 30 days after the subprocessor list is updated. If the parties cannot resolve the objection in good faith, Customer may stop using the affected part of the Service and terminate the affected subscription in accordance with the Agreement.

## 8. Assistance with Data Subject Requests

Taking into account the nature of the processing, Notra will provide reasonable assistance to Customer through appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, to help Customer respond to requests from data subjects exercising rights under Data Protection Laws.

Where a data subject sends a request directly to Notra concerning Customer Personal Data, Notra may direct the data subject to Customer unless legally required to respond directly.

## 9. Security Incident Notification

Notra will notify Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a Security Incident involving Customer Personal Data.

The notice will include information reasonably available to Notra, such as the nature of the incident, affected data categories, likely consequences, measures taken or proposed to address the incident, and a contact point for follow-up questions.

Notra's notification of a Security Incident is not an acknowledgement of fault or liability.

## 10. Assistance with Compliance

Taking into account the nature of the processing and information available to Notra, Notra will provide reasonable assistance to Customer with Customer's obligations under Data Protection Laws relating to security, breach notification, data protection impact assessments, and consultation with supervisory authorities.

## 11. Return and Deletion

During the subscription term, Customer may access, export, or delete Customer Personal Data through the Service where available.

After termination or expiry of the Agreement, Notra will delete or return Customer Personal Data in accordance with the Agreement, product functionality, backup retention practices, and applicable law. Unless otherwise required by law, Notra will delete Customer Personal Data within 30 days after account deletion or termination of the affected Service, except for data retained in backups, logs, billing records, security records, or legal records according to Notra's retention practices.

Backup copies are protected from production use and are deleted on their normal lifecycle.

## 12. Audits and Information

Notra will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

Customer may request an audit of Notra's compliance with this DPA no more than once per calendar year, unless required by a supervisory authority or following a Security Incident affecting Customer Personal Data. Audits must be conducted during normal business hours, with reasonable advance notice, by Customer or an independent auditor subject to confidentiality obligations, and in a manner that does not unreasonably disrupt Notra's operations or compromise the security or confidentiality of other customers.

Notra may satisfy an audit request by providing relevant documentation, security summaries, third-party certifications, questionnaires, or written responses, where appropriate.

## 13. International Transfers

Customer acknowledges that Notra and its subprocessors may process Customer Personal Data in the United States, European Union, and other locations described on the subprocessor page or otherwise made available by Notra.

Where Customer Personal Data protected by the GDPR, UK GDPR, or Swiss data protection law is transferred to a country that does not provide an adequate level of protection, the parties agree that the SCCs are incorporated into this DPA as follows:

- Module Two applies where Customer is a controller and Notra is a processor.
- Module Three applies where Customer is a processor and Notra is a subprocessor.
- Clause 7, the optional docking clause, applies.
- For Clause 9, option 2 applies, and the time period for prior notice of subprocessor changes is 30 days.
- For Clause 11, the optional language does not apply.
- For Clause 17, the SCCs are governed by the laws of Germany.
- For Clause 18, disputes under the SCCs will be resolved by the courts of Germany.
- Annexes I, II, and III of the SCCs are completed by Annexes 1, 2, and 3 of this DPA.

For transfers subject to the UK GDPR, the SCCs are modified by the UK International Data Transfer Addendum or any replacement mechanism approved under UK law. For transfers subject to Swiss law, the SCCs apply with the modifications required by Swiss data protection law.

## 14. United States Privacy Laws

Where Notra processes Customer Personal Data subject to applicable United States privacy laws, Notra will process that data as a service provider, processor, or equivalent role. Notra will not sell or share Customer Personal Data, retain, use, or disclose Customer Personal Data outside the business purposes described in this DPA and the Agreement, or combine Customer Personal Data with personal data obtained from other sources except as permitted by applicable law.

## 15. Order of Precedence

If there is a conflict between this DPA and the Agreement, this DPA controls with respect to the processing of Customer Personal Data. If there is a conflict between this DPA and the SCCs, the SCCs control to the extent required by applicable Data Protection Laws.

## Annex 1: Details of Processing

**Subject matter:** Notra's provision of the Service to Customer, including account management, workspace collaboration, connected integrations, content ingestion, AI-assisted content generation, workflow automation, API access, billing, support, analytics, and security operations.

**Duration:** For the term of the Agreement and until Customer Personal Data is deleted or returned in accordance with this DPA and the Agreement.

**Nature and purpose of processing:** Collection, receipt, access, storage, organization, retrieval, transmission, analysis, transformation, generation, display, export, restriction, deletion, and other processing necessary to provide and secure the Service.

**Categories of data subjects:** Customer's users, employees, contractors, representatives, workspace members, invited collaborators, customers, prospects, integration users, end users, and individuals whose information is included in Customer content or connected third-party services.

**Categories of Customer Personal Data:** Names, email addresses, account identifiers, organization and workspace identifiers, authentication data, profile data, role and permission data, API key metadata, billing and subscription metadata, usage logs, support communications, IP addresses, device and browser metadata, prompts, generated content, uploaded content, connected integration data, repository metadata, issue metadata, messages, comments, documentation, website content, and other data submitted to or generated through the Service.

**Special categories of data:** The Service is not designed for processing special categories of personal data. Customer must not submit special categories of personal data unless expressly permitted by the Agreement and supported by an appropriate legal basis.

**Frequency of transfer:** Continuous or as initiated by Customer's use and configuration of the Service.

**Processing locations:** United States, European Union, and other locations used by Notra and its subprocessors as described in this DPA and the subprocessor list.

## Annex 2: Technical and Organizational Measures

Notra maintains technical and organizational measures designed to protect Customer Personal Data, including:

- access controls based on least privilege, role-based authorization, and account-level authentication;
- encryption in transit using HTTPS/TLS;
- encryption at rest where supported by infrastructure providers;
- secure handling of secrets, credentials, API keys, and integration tokens;
- logical separation of customer workspaces and organization data;
- audit, application, and infrastructure logging for security, debugging, and operational monitoring;
- backup and recovery practices appropriate to the hosted infrastructure used by the Service;
- vulnerability management through dependency updates, code review, and platform security controls;
- production access limited to personnel and service accounts with a business need;
- security monitoring, alerting, and incident response processes;
- data minimization in analytics and operational monitoring where feasible;
- deletion and retention controls for account data, workspace data, logs, and backups;
- confidentiality obligations for personnel with access to Customer Personal Data;
- vendor and subprocessor review before engaging providers that may process Customer Personal Data.

The current list of subprocessors is available at [usenotra.com/subprocessors](/subprocessors).

The list identifies subprocessors, their processing purposes, processing locations, and websites. Customer authorizes Notra to use those subprocessors for the purposes described in this DPA.
